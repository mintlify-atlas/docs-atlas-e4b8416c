---
title: "Rendering text and typography with GPT Image 2"
description: "How to reliably render in-image text with GPT Image 2: quote required strings in your prompt and use quality=high for legible results in any language."
---

GPT Image 2 can render in-image text — slogans, prices, labels, kanji — with high accuracy when you follow two rules. Both rules are required; skipping either one produces unreliable or illegible results, especially for non-Latin scripts and small type sizes.

## Rule 1: Put required text in straight quotes

Wrap every string that must appear verbatim in your image in straight quotation marks (`"`). This signals to the model that the enclosed characters are literal copy to render, not thematic inspiration. Without quotes, the model may paraphrase, omit, or stylise the text.

**Without quotes (unreliable):**
```text
Design a tea poster with the headline Mountain River Tea and price 16 yuan
```

**With quotes (reliable):**
```text
Design a tea poster. Exact copy: "山川茶事" as the headline, "冷泡系列" as the subtitle, "中杯 16 元" as the price.
```

<Tip>
  Use the word `Exact copy:` before listing quoted strings. It reinforces to the model that these are literal rendering targets, not stylistic suggestions.
</Tip>

## Rule 2: Always use `--quality high` for images with text

Text rendering degrades visibly at the default quality level. Characters become blurry, strokes merge, and small labels become illegible. Set `--quality high` (or `quality="high"` in the SDK) for any image that contains in-image text.

This applies to:
- Latin text at any size
- CJK characters (Chinese, Japanese, Korean)
- Prices, dates, and numeric labels
- Multi-line copy or dense typographic layouts
- Small captions or watermark-style text

## Example: Chinese tea poster

### Prompt

```text
Design a 3:4 portrait tea poster. Soft green watercolour wash background, minimal layout, generous whitespace. Exact copy: "山川茶事" as the large headline at top, "冷泡系列" centred below it, "中杯 16 元" at the bottom as the price. Traditional brush-style typeface.
```

### CLI command

```bash
gpt-image -p 'Design a 3:4 portrait tea poster. Soft green watercolour wash background, minimal layout, generous whitespace. Exact copy: "山川茶事" as the large headline at top, "冷泡系列" centred below it, "中杯 16 元" at the bottom as the price. Traditional brush-style typeface.' \
  --size portrait --quality high -f poster.png
```

<Note>
  The outer prompt is wrapped in single quotes so that the inner straight double-quotes are passed to the model without shell escaping. Alternatively, escape them with backslashes: `\"`.
</Note>

## Why quality matters for text

At the default quality setting the model allocates fewer diffusion steps to fine detail. For photographic scenes this is often acceptable. For text, where every pixel in a character stroke carries meaning, the reduction is immediately visible:

| Quality | Result |
| --- | --- |
| `low` or `medium` | Characters may blur, merge, or be misshapen at small sizes |
| `high` | Strokes are sharp, characters are individually legible, CJK glyphs render correctly |

<Warning>
  Even at `quality=high`, very small point sizes (equivalent to under ~12pt in the output image) can still be unreliable. Keep required copy at a reasonable display size and avoid long strings of fine print.
</Warning>
