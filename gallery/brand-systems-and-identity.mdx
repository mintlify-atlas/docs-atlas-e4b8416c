GPT Image 2 can generate cohesive brand identity assets — logo lockups with variations, annotated color palette cards, and realistic business card mockups. The prompts below demonstrate three presentation formats used in real identity projects: a full brand sheet with logo anatomy and usage examples, a standalone color and typography card, and a physical card mockup in perspective. Providing hex codes, typeface names, and explicit layout sections gives the model the specificity it needs to produce a presentation-quality result.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Coffee roastery brand sheet" description="Size: landscape · Quality: high · Source: Curated">
    ```
    A brand identity sheet for a fictional specialty coffee roastery called "Anchor & Bean". Left side: primary logo — an anchor with a coffee bean integrated into the shank, wordmark in a warm humanist sans-serif below. Right side: logo variations (dark, light, monochrome), brand colors (deep navy #1B2A4A, warm cream #F5EDD3, roast amber #C4622D), typeface specimen, and usage examples on packaging. Clean white background, editorial presentation.
    ```

    ```bash CLI
    gpt-image -p "A brand identity sheet for a fictional specialty coffee roastery called \"Anchor & Bean\". Left side: primary logo — an anchor with a coffee bean integrated into the shank, wordmark in a warm humanist sans-serif below. Right side: logo variations (dark, light, monochrome), brand colors (deep navy #1B2A4A, warm cream #F5EDD3, roast amber #C4622D), typeface specimen, and usage examples on packaging. Clean white background, editorial presentation." \
      --size 1536x1024 \
      --quality high \
      -f anchor-bean-brand-sheet.png
    ```
  </Accordion>

<Tip>
  Specifying exact hex codes for brand colors anchors the palette and prevents the model from drifting toward generic hues. For logo sheets, listing the exact variation names you need ("dark, light, monochrome") in the prompt ensures each appears in the output.
</Tip>
